# Ana G. Chavez

**Computational Neuroscientist · Baylor College of Medicine**

I study how the human hippocampus organizes semantic content during naturalistic conversation — and how that geometry mirrors the hidden-state structure of large language models.

---

### Research

**Mirror Manifolds** · *Nature Communications, under review*
Hippocampal neurons encode word meaning in semi-orthogonal subspaces that simultaneously generalize and differentiate across speakers — across 10 patients and 442 single units during free conversation. [[bioRxiv](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.09.20.677504v2)]

**Trajectory Curvature & LLM Alignment** · *submitted, NeurIPS 2026*
Hippocampal trajectory curvature tracks semantic surprisal and predicts cross-modal decoding performance — identifying curvature as the dominant geometric bridge between brain and LLM representations.

**24/7 Foundation Model** · *ongoing*
Collabing for the scaling of the semantic encoding framework to continuous intracranial recordings across a patient's full hospital stay — 20× the data scale of prior work.
