About

Bridging brains and
artificial intelligence

I am a pre-doctoral researcher at Baylor College of Medicine working with rare human single-unit intracranial recordings from the hippocampus and ACC during naturalistic conversation.

My research revealed that hippocampal population geometry mirrors LLM hidden-state structure — suggesting biological and artificial intelligence converge on shared representational solutions for semantic processing.

I am a Biomedical Informatics and Data Science NLM fellow with a track record of cross-disciplinary collaboration and national science communication.

Research

Selected projects

Nature Communications · Under Review

Mirror Manifolds: Shared Semantic Geometry for Speaking & Listening

Hippocampal neurons encode word meaning in semi-orthogonal subspaces that simultaneously generalize and differentiate across speakers — a new solution to the cross-speaker representational problem. Across 10 patients and 442 neurons.

Read preprint

Submitted · NeurIPS 2026

Trajectory Curvature as the Geometric Bridge Between Brains and LLMs

Hippocampal trajectory curvature tracks semantic surprisal and predicts cross-modal decoding performance. Curvature is the dominant geometric metric aligning hippocampal and LLM representations — with direct implications for few-shot semantic speech BCI decoding.

Foundation Model · Ongoing

Continuous Semantic Encoding: 24/7 Foundation Model

Scaling the encoding framework 20× to continuous 24/7 intracranial recordings — one of the first efforts to model semantic neural dynamics at the timescale of real human experience rather than controlled laboratory conditions.

Multi-center · NeurIPS Manuscript

AI-Assisted EEG Analysis of Live Musical Improvisation

Resident computational neuroscientist on a BCM–UH–Rice collaboration. Processing multimodal naturalistic data (EEG, IMU, EOG) from participants engaged in live music, dance, and creative performance. Identified neural markers for a CNN-based decoding model.
